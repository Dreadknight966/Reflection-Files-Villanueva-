Reflection
Republic Act 10175 known as the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 which was enacted due to the increasing cybercrimes in the Philippines. This law aims to protect individuals who may fall victim to scams, hacking and identify theft, as well as punishing individuals who may partake in illegal activities online such as distributing illegal pornography or selling of illegal items: Weapons, Drugs, Human Trafficking. The act discusses punishments for individuals found guilty of committing cybercrimes depending on the severity of the crime as well as the accountability for the criminal in question.

Definition of various cybercrimes

  • Illegal access / Hacking: Unauthorized access to a computers systems and it's networks.
  • Data Interference: Damaging, altering or deleting data on a system without permission or authorization.
  • Identity theft: Acting as another person using their information to gain more information or commit illegal acts.
  • Illegal pornography: The distribution or production of sexually explicit content involving minors online.

The PNP anti-cybercrime group is the enforcing group of republic act 10175. They actively investigate and prevent cybercrime using their extensive network and members in order to protect the users who are oblivious to such acts going on in the background. They Coordinate with international enforcement agencies in order to apprehend criminals across borders. They upgrade their hardware and infrastructure in order to stay ahead and compete with the evolving threats that may happen at any moment. They train their forces in order to have expertise to stay effective as well as hire experts.
